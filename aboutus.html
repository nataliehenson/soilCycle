About Us


Soil Cycle is a composting and organic gardening initiative focused on promoting waste reduction, food security, and sustainability, through education and community engagement. We seek to divert organic waste and building materials from landfills in order to facilitate soil regeneration and grow food using systems that do so not only carbon neutrally, but carbon negatively.

By partnering with Denverâ€™s Mile High 360, a youth resource non-profit, Soil Cycle diverts organic waste from local businesses via bicycle, and uses reclaimed building materials to build and maintain an educational garden. The students at Mile High 360 also participate in community engagement projects and attend service learning trips to work in environments, which broaden their view of sustainability and food access.

The systems shared with students, families, and our community are designed to promote a sustainable future, and to do so in a way that provides food and financial security.
